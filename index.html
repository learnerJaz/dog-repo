The Issue in India

Many dogs live on the streets with limited access to food, vaccination, and sterilization. Public debates can become polarized: remove dogs vs. leave them on the streets. Both miss the point: the humane, proven path is to reduce suffering and improve public health through vaccination and spay/neuter programs, supported shelters, responsible adoption, and community education.

Practical Solutions We Can Start Today

  1. Vaccinate: Coordinate with local vets/NGOs for anti-rabies drives.
  2. Spay/Neuter: Prioritize high-density areas; track coverage.
  3. Feed Responsibly: Fixed, clean spots; remove leftovers; avoid crowding.
  4. Adopt/Foster: Promote adoption over buying; support foster networks.
  5. Report Cruelty: Document, call helplines, and follow legal routes.

Understanding & Caring for Dogs

Basic Needs

  • Food: Plain rice, roti, boiled vegetables, eggs, and dog food are safe. Avoid chocolate, onions, grapes, and fried/spicy foods.
  • Water: Always keep a bowl of clean water in shady spots, especially during summer.
  • Shelter: A simple shade or box protects from rain and heat.

How Dogs React

  • When Hungry: Dogs may follow people, bark more, or scavenge. Regular feeding reduces stress and aggression.
  • When Afraid: They may tuck their tails, avoid eye contact, or bark defensively. Stay calm and don‚Äôt chase them.
  • When Friendly: Wagging tails, relaxed posture, and gentle approaches mean they trust you.

Community Tips

  • Feed at fixed places and times to avoid crowding and fights.
  • Coordinate with neighbors to share responsibility ‚Äî not just one person.
  • Get street dogs vaccinated and neutered with local NGOs or vets.
  • Introduce children gently to dogs; teach them not to throw stones or tease.
  • Call a vet/NGO if you see injuries, illness, or cruelty cases.
